Kidney 4 Kelly

-- Meet Kelly --

I have always been a private person when it came to my medical challenges, and it is difficult for me to ask for help. However, I am now in need of someone to step forward to become my living donor.


As many of you know, I've had diabetes for the majority of my life and a complication over the last few years has been that my kidneys are failing. My doctors said that my best option is to find a living kidney donor. If you're intested in learning more or helping me, please take a quick look at the site and potentially even registering to be tested.


I know this is something incredible to ask, and I'm fortunate enough to have incredible people in my life who want to help, but finding a matching donor is difficult. Even if you are unable to help yourself, please share my story and the link to this site so that I can find that incredible person.


Thank you so much, even just visiting this site means the world to me.


-- Do I Qualify? --

If you meet the below criteria you qualify to be tested and potentially donate to help Kelly:


  • Be between the ages of 18 and 75
  • Be in general good health
  • A blood type match is NOT required!
  • Not required to be related
  • Have an uneslfish desire to contribute to another person's life in a healthy way

-- How to Donate --

If you're interested in donating your kidney, please follow these simple steps:


  1. Fill out the Living Donor Registration Form with the following information:
    • What Organ: KIDNEY
    • Donation to: SPECIFIC INDIVIDUAL
    • Recipient a UPMC Patient: YES
    • Child or Adult: ADULT
    • Recipient Name: Kelly Sabol

  2. After completing the online registration, you should be contacted within 3 buisness days.
    • Please try to have your voice mail set up and able to accept messages!
    • If you have not heard from UPMC within 3 business days please call 412-647-5800 and ask for the Living Donor Office.

  3. For more information visit Living Donor Kidney or call 412-647-5800

-- Questions --

  1. Will Kelly know any of my personl information if I register to help her?
    • UPMC protects the privacy of all living donors throught the entire process, so Kelly is not allowed to be given any information about who registers to help her.
    • You are free to contact Kelly and speak with her about the process, but UPMC will not inform her of the status of any potential donors until a match is found.
    • If you are found to be a match and willing to donate to Kelly, YOU will have the honor and responsiblity of notififying her before UPMC will move forward with the process.

  2. Will I have to cover the cost of being tested?
    • All costs of testing (except for routine cancer screenings) will be covered by Kelly's insurance.

  3. What if I can't afford to travel or take the time off to donate?
    • The National Living Donor Assistance Center (NLDAC) can help cover the cost of travel, hotel stays, meals, and lost wages up to $6000.
    • It is a felony for Kelly or any of her loved ones to offer money/goods/services in exchange for a donation. A donation must be made by an individual motivated by purely selfless intentions who is interested in saving a life.

  4. What would life be like after I donate to help Kelly?
    • Life for a living donor is largely unaffected as long as they continue to lead a healthy lifestyle. You may need to limit intake of things like alcohol or certain pain medications, but only avoiding using them in excess.
    • Your chances of developing things like kidney disease yourself, or even high blood pressure remain unafftected by being a living donor.

  5. What do I need to know about the surgery and post-op process?
    • The surgery for the donor is minimially-invasive, only lasts a couple hours, involves a small 4 inch incision, and leaves little to no scarring.
    • In the entire history of UPMC, a living donor has NEVER died as part of the donation process.
    • You can be discharged in 1 day after surgery.
    • You will need a caregiver to keep an eye on you after surgery, as there is a 6 week recovery period where you should avoid lifting anything heavy.
